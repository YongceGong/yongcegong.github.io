About Me

I am a master’s student in astrophysics in a joint program between Guangzhou University and Great Bay University. My research focuses on cosmic-ray simulations,FLUKA Monte Carlo simulation, detector-related studies, neutron and muon measurements, scientific data analysis, and near infrared color-color analysis of dense molecular cloud cores.

My interests include astroparticle physics, Monte Carlo simulations for detectors and cosmic rays, detector optimization, and experimental studies connected to particle and radiation detection. This website provides a concise overview of my background, current work, selected projects, talks, and professional links.

Selected Projects

Localized Cosmic-Ray and Atmospheric Cascade Simulation with FLUKA

Performed FLUKA simulations of localized cosmic rays, obtaining the muon zenith-angle distribution, neutron energy spectrum, muon energy spectrum, and east-west effect.

Muon Lifetime Studies in EJ-200 Plastic Scintillators

Simulated the decay of cosmic-ray muons in EJ-200 plastic scintillators and analyzed lifetime distributions, using FLUKA user routines to record decay information for study.

Neutron Detector Design and Optimization

Participated in the design of heavy-water-moderated ³He+CF₄ scintillation neutron detectors, optimized a ³He polyethylene-moderated neutron detector, and contributed to detector-related work connected to CSNS experiments.
